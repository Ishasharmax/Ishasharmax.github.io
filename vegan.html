Veganxzer is an vegan grocery delivery app, which strives to deliver its users fresh groceries, fastly and conveniently. Through this app, users can also explore various vegan recipes daily.







Duration

February - March 2022

Role

UX/UI Designer

Responsibilities

User Research, Moodboard, Personas, User Journey Map, User Flow, Wireframes, Lo-fi and Hi-fi Prototypes, Design Principles, Market Research, User Testing

Tools

Adobe Xd, Figma, Photoshop

Problems

- People with busy schedules have hard time buying groceries in-person.
- Emerging vegans don't know a lot about vegan foods and recipes.
- Health conscious people can't get information about food's calorie and nutritional content.
- People allergic to some allergins have hard time finding suitable recipes.


Goals

- To design an app for people who want to explore the vegan foods and recipes.
- Emerging vegans don't know a lot about vegan foods and recipes.

LESSONS LEARNED

- Developing a mobile app UX/UI on lapton can lead to making sizing errors. Fortunately, usability testing can be performed to minimize and rectify such errors.
- It is crucial to maintain a robust yet soothing color palette throughout the creation, to make it readable for the user.
- When immersing oneself in creating a case study, it is a good idea to have a schedule in mind, and complete the tasks at a given moment to reduce spending time in unplanned details.

NEXT STEPS

There is always room left for improvement, it is true for this study as well. Since, I completed this study by myself with the insight of people I interviewed and the personas I created, there must be a lot more perspectives not taken into account that could make this app an even more accessible and successful one.

As for now, all I've invisioned with this app is to add custom delivery, so the user would have fexibilty in when (date and time) their food can be delivered to them.
